Project Summary

Fruits and vegetables are an important part of our daily food and are essential for good health. But nowadays, many of them are grown or stored using harmful chemicals like pesticides and ripening agents. These chemicals can affect our health and reduce the quality of food. People often consume them without knowing the risks involved.

As part of our Community Service Project, we focused on creating awareness about the chemicals used in fruits and vegetables. Many farmers and vendors use these chemicals to increase yield or shelf life. In villages and small towns, people are not aware of these harmful effects and continue to consume such produce.

Our aim is to educate people on the dangers of these chemicals and promote better alternatives like natural cleaning methods and organic farming practices. Spreading awareness and encouraging safe food habits can improve public health and lead to a healthier lifestyle in rural areas.

Key Focus Areas

  • Chemical contamination in fruits and vegetables
  • Health risks associated with pesticides and ripening agents
  • Lack of awareness in rural communities
  • Promoting natural cleaning methods
  • Encouraging organic farming practices
  • Improving public health through education
